Supreme Court judgement on Basic Wages

The Supreme Court passed the order dated 28. 02. 2019 in five connected matters thus settling, at least for the time being, the position as to ‘basic wages’ under the E. P. F. Act, 1952. Surprisingly, however, there still exists confusion as regards understanding the said judgement. I have received many queries related thereto, hence I have, hereunder, answered them as precisely as possible.

1. What attracts EPF contribution?

Answer: Any component that is paid to all in any given category (paid ordinarily, necessarily and universally) attracts EPF contribution.

2. What does not attract EPF contribution?

Answer: Any component which is paid as incentive for extra work or output and actual reimbursement shall not attract EPF contribution.

3. What is the significance of this judgement?

Answer: The judgment interprets the provision, hence it lays down the law as to basic wages. Department shall act upon it more vigorously now. Further, the department vide its circular dated 14.03.2019 has called for reinforcement of the aforesaid position. Even inspections for the prior period may be based on this interpretation.

4. What is the date of its applicability?

Answer: There is no question of any date of applicability as this is an interpretation of an existing provision, not a notification. It’s application is perpetual subject to overriding conditions such as a future judgement overruling it or a larger bench holding a different position or an amendment to the provision itself.

5. Whether this interpretation applies to Gratuity or Bonus calculations?

Answer: This interpretation is of ‘Basic Wages’ under the EPF Act, hence it may not apply to Gratuity or Bonus calculation.

6. Does this judgement have any bearing on employees getting ‘basic wages’ more than 15,000/- ?

Answer: This judgement has no bearing on employees whose ‘basic wages’ exceeds 15,000/-(however, revision of this ceiling is under consideration). Contribution paid by Employer on any amount exceeding the said ceiling, being voluntary, may not be subjected to scrutiny for the purpose of making contribution under this Act.( Please refer to the Proviso to Para 26A of the EPF Scheme 1952).

7. Is HRA excluded from the purview of ‘basic wages’?

Answer: Some confusing materials saying that ‘HRA is excluded’ are in circulation. To clear the confusion, I must base my answer on the judgement itself. Kindly read the judgement (only two parts thereof) to understand the position yourself. Please go to Para 2 of the judgement comprising recitals of the connected matters in which the present judgement has been passed. Now please read the third entry:- “Civil Appeal Nos. 3969-70 of 2013: The appellant was not deducting Provident Fund contribution on house rent allowance, special allowance, management allowance and conveyance allowance by excluding it from basic wage. The authority under the Act held that the allowances had to be taken into account as basic wage for deduction. The High Court dismissed the writ petition and the review petition filed by the appellant”.

Hope you have read ‘HRA’ in the very second sentence of the reproduced para. Also that the Employer (U Flex) in this matter would not deduct EPF on HRA among others. The department held that HRA, among others, had to be treated a part of the ‘basic wages’. Having lost to the department before the High Court, the Employer approached the Supreme Court by way of C.A. no. 3969-70 of 2013.

Now please read the last para no. 15 of the judgement:- “15. Resultantly, Civil Appeal No. 6221 of 2011 is allowed. Civil Appeal Nos. 3965-66 of 2013, Civil Appeal Nos. 3967-68 of 2013, Civil Appeal Nos. 3969-70 of 2013 and Transfer Case (C) No.19 of 2019 are dismissed.” The second last C.A. No. 3969-70 of 2013 was dismissed, among others, by the Supreme Court without any exception or qualification. Thus leading to the position that HRA is not excluded from ‘basic wages’. I must clarify further that para 5 to 7 of the judgement merely deal with the submissions made by the parties and do not lay down the position.

All probable question are accordingly answered.

Satish Kumar Jha

Advocate
